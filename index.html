Rock, Paper, Scissors Rules:

  1. Rock beats Scissors
  2. Scissors beats Paper
  3. Paper beats Rock

Each player picks one option, and the winner is determined based on these simple interactions. If both players choose the same, it's a tie!

Rock, Paper, Scissors is a simple hand game with ancient roots, believed to have originated in China over 2,000 years ago, known as "shoushiling." The game spread to Japan, where it became popular as "jan-ken" with the familiar rock, paper, and scissors gestures.
